UWMCC Rebranding

One of the first on-campus clubs I was involved in is The University of Waterloo’s Management Consulting Club (UWMCC), a club that seeks attract students and help them acquire the basic skills needed to succeed in pursuing opportunities in the consulting industry.

Having been involved for four consecutive terms (as a marketing director, graphic designer, VP marketing, and now an advisor) I’ve seen and designed plenty of graphics for the club. As the club’s events are well known, I felt that it be important to establish a strong brand presence.

Annual Case Competition


UWMCC holds an annual case competition where students from all universities can compete. Winter 2018 was my first term on the team, as a marketing director I provided feedback for designs and helped to promote events on social media.

In Winter 2019, I was VP Marketing where I was responsible for creating all graphic deliverables. Following the branding guide and using the previous year’s graphic for reference, I used Photoshop and Illustrator to create the following graphics.

Countdown social media posts

A simple countdown graphic to help promote on social media and increase engagement. To determine what content to include, I surveyed and asked a few friends for their opinion. The majority agreed that keeping it as simple as possible and including the attending firms were most important.

Nametags

We thought designing different name tags was the best method for attendants to distinguish between themselves. Judges had a fancy geometric background and unique layout whereas team members and delegates had the same layout but opposite color scheme. So team members could be found with ease, their nametags had a teal background to stand out.

Taking into consideration of survey answers I resized the poster to create a Facebook page banner.

Event graphics

As per the previous marketing guide UWMCC wanted a crisp and modern aesthetic, using geometric or business backgrounds, thin lines, banners, and the UWMCC teal. Keeping this in mind, I designed my first event graphic for the club.

Exploring aesthetics

I felt that the previous graphics didn’t represent the modern brand that UWMCC wanted to portray. The teal shade was unsaturated. When used as an overlay it made background images dim, as shown in the Tech Consulting Day social media posts.

While I felt the use of stock images delivered the purpose of the event well, the first thing I wanted to change was the use of unsaturated teal overlays and images as backgrounds as I felt it made the graphic seem clustered and messy. I also explored using vibrant teals/blues for emphasis.

Afterwards, I thought of how I can correlate graphics better with the consulting industry and what better way to represent the industry than to incorporate a PowerPoint style layout!

Although the new designs were preferred, the feedback mentioned that the light colors didn’t allow the post to stand out when a user scrolls through their timeline. Taking this into consideration I switched up the color scheme again and played around with gradients, which is a current design trend I personally love.

Using geometric shapes, as per the previous branding guidelines, and vibrant gradients.

To avoid straying too far from the logo colors, I used a more saturated and brighter shade of teal.
