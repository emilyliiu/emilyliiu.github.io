Lumos Flux
Landing page design


Worked with a medical wearable technology start-up to rapidly design their landing page for CES 2020.



Platform:
Web

Tools:
Figma, Photoshop

Deliverables:
Webpage design

Timeframe:
2 months

Context:

Company background

Approximately 100,000 years ago, night owls served the purpose of defending the tribes against predators in the dark. They were known as the sentinels, and their cells have evolved over thousands of years with a mutation in the CLOCK gene.

40%
of the population today still carries that mutation.

However, the modern world has changed to a 9-5 schedule, and night owls are put at a disadvantage, as they are forced to follow a routine that is unaligned with their genetic clock. As a result, many night owls suffer from sleep deprivation, and are at higher risks for depression, insomnia, and heart disease.

My role

Designing the landing page

I was brought on to help with designing the Lumos Flux landing page in preparation for CES 2020. As Lumos Flux was in its very early stages of development (working on pushing out a MVP at the time), there wasn’t much design direction for me to follow. I was responsible for researching competitors and determining the visual look and feel.

Deliverable 1:

Color pallet

We wanted to position the Lumos Flux product as an everyday type of glasses, where it would be normal to see someone walk down the street wearing them. On the other hand, we also wanted to capture the market segment of customers that are looking for a high-tech solution to cure their sleep patterns.

Landing page wireframe

Light mode

A more professional look and feel that's will be the standard webpage.



Dark mode

Dark theme will be easier on the eyes when browsing late at night, perfect for the night owls.

Reflection:

Predicting technical constraints

Working with a team of devs I learned more about how to better communicate with them, how to ask for input, and forecast what iterations are within their capabilities to decide between the nice-to-have features from core functionalities.



Working within tight deadlines

Adapting a mentality of quickly pushing out design ideas, getting feedback, iterate or scrap, and repeat. This opportunity allowed me to work in an environment that fostered consistent feedback, from both users and the team.
