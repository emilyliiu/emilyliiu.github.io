Yong Funds Program
Website Redesign


This website redesign was our final deliverable for a course. During the 3-month process, the 4 of us critiqued and presented a research-driven website redesign to educate young adults on financial literacy.



Platform:
Desktop

Tools:
Adobe XD, Photoshop, HTML/CSS

Deliverables:
User Research, UX/UI Design, Hi-fidelity Mockup

My role:
UX Researcher, Developer

A familiar problem

More than three-quarters of students (approx. 76%) report that they wish they had more help preparing for their personal finances. Young Funds Program (YFP for short) is a student-run initiative aimed towards developing financial literacy, specifically investing experience, in young adults.

Defining the audience

We already know that YFP’s purpose is to educate young adults on financial literacy, specifically the basics of investing. To get a better understanding of exactly who we’re designing for, I interviewed and complied data on my friends. I wanted to get insight on what would they want to see based on their current level of financial literacy.

Interview findings

We timed how long users spent on each page as, according to HubSpot, young adults typically spend less than 15 seconds on a website. When interviewees went through the site, we asked questions such as:

  • What’s your next step from here?
  • What were you hoping to get from this page?
  • Is there anything that stands out to you?
  • Did you learn anything from this page?

The pain points

After individually critiquing the website, we asked two from each audience segment for their opinion as well. We concluded the main pain points to be:

1. Outdated look/feel

Screencapture of the current landing page.

2. Unorganized content

Note the unbalanced margins and inconsistent design choices.

3. Navigation difficulties

Duplication of nested navigation menu in the footer

What do we do?

As someone who fell into the target demographic (intrigued by investing but unsure how to start), I was eager to help. Our goal was to present a redesign of the current YFP website, with regards to their three primary goals. For each goal, we brainstormed leading questions on why it matters to the audience, how is it currently being achieved, and how it can be improved.

Categorizing YFP's 3 primary goals to brainstorm follow-up questions and benchmark current performance.

Information architecture

The first step was to produce a sitemap, targeting pain points #1 and #2. The goal was to map out the information architecture and how to guide user flow.

Breakdown of the information architecture

Wireframes - part 1

We individually developed low-fidelity wireframes for each page. The challenge was how to create an improved platform that brings value to all three audience segments, while keeping in mind of YFP’s goals. To remain within the 15 second metric and using findings from interviews, I designed a landing page that shows a high-level overview of everything YFP encompasses.

First iteration of low-fidelity wireframes

Coincidentally, what interviewees ranked as most important content aligned with YFP’s goals. They were interested in the offerings (Goal #2) and the success metrics, which boosts accountability and thus increase the likelihood of user engagement (Goal #1).

Interviews showed users were most interested in what the ‘Contents’ page offered, therefore a CTA to that page was added.

To increase user engagement (Goal #1) a CTA to the ‘Contact Us’ page was added at the bottom. This was also done to prevent the user experience from ending awkwardly with the user being confused with what to do next.

Final tweaks before development

We conducted a few rounds of user testing and made changes accordingly. The main feedback points we recieved was that there is too much going on, testers were unsure which part of the site to focus on first and kept getting distracted by other elements. Based on the feedback, we made the following changes:

  • Tone down the neon green to darker forest green, used sparingly to add emphasis rather than as backgrounds
  • Increase padding to separate sections and child elements to improve readability and hierarchy
  • Simplified navigation menu by removing duplicate social media icons

Final wireframe designs to be handed off for development. Some minor tweaks in color and spacing consistencies were made when coding the site.
