Background

The motivation behind this project was for one of my second-year classes, where teams of four worked together to redesign and develop a website for a local organization. Luckily, one of my friends runs an initiative, Young Funds Program (YFP), and was looking to revamp the website. YFP aims to develop financial literacy amongst young adults through offering services, such as workshops and personal consultation.

Distribution of work

We individually completed a critique of the current website, wireframes, and presented our recommendations to one another for feedback. After sharing and discussing our findings in terms of usability, design, and content, we split into pairs.

Nish and Sakksham were responsible for creating the final wireframes, deciding design aspects (font and color pallet), and creating the final presentation.

Gina and I split leadership over the project, finalized the prototype for development, and developed the site using HTML/CSS and bits of Javascript.

Main pain points:

  1. Navigation difficulties: the main feedback we received was that it’s difficult to navigate through the site as information was scattered all over the page and call to action items were unclear. Users also noted that the menu included too many links, making it unclear as to what YFP is.
  2. Content disorganization: students found that it was difficult to understand the purpose of the website due to a lack of hierarchy between groups of contents. Additionally, there weren’t clear headings or spacing between different sections of content, making it difficult for the user to understand the message YFP is trying to convey.
  3. Overall aesthetics: there was a lack of consistency throughout the site and the use of purple and green clashed. Furthermore, the use of animations didn’t add to the design, instead it was distracting. Overall, the site didn’t appear to be very professional.

The design process

Ideation

Working closely with Travis allowed us to get a good understanding of how to successfully cater the redesign to the target audience. With this in mind, I researched the layout and common themes between other finance and educational service websites. As young adults typically scan rather than read, I focused on re-organizing content so that a user can easily understand the purpose and offerings YFP provides when scrolling through the main page. Clear call to action buttons is included at almost every content block so that if the user is interested, they can easily click to learn more. The homepage also ends with a call to action for the user to sign-up as the purpose of the website is to both educate and connect with potential clients.

Wireframes pt. 1

The biggest challenge I had was determining how to organize content to reduce repeated information and creating a layout that allows for easy information delivery to the user. I began by researching other educational/portfolio management sites to get a better understanding of the way they organized information and the common layouts. Afterwards, I created wireframes using Adobe XD to showcase the basic layout for the site.
